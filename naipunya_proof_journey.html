Step 1
12 × 14 = 168. Build it.
12 rows × 14 columns
Each small square = 1 unit²
12 × 14 = 168
Step 2
Rewrite 12 and 14 around their midpoint: 13
12 = 13 − 1
14 = 13 + 1
So: 12 × 14 = (13 − 1)(13 + 1)
Step 3
Expand (13 − 1)(13 + 1) — term by term
①13 → ②13 13 × 13 = 169
①13 → ②+1 13 × 1 = +13
①−1 → ②13 −1 × 13 = −13
①−1 → ②+1 −1 × 1 = −1
✕ +13 and −13 cancel each other out. They disappear.
Step 4
What's left after cancellation?
(13 − 1)(13 + 1)
= 169 + +13 − 13 − 1
↑ these cancel each other out
= 169 − 1
= 168 ✓
13² − 1²
Big square (169) minus corner (1) = 168
Step 5
Replace 13 with a and 1 with b. Does it still work?
(a − b)(a + b)
= a² + ab − ab − b²
= a² − b²
Always. The middle terms always cancel.
(a − b)(a + b) = a² − b²
This works for any two numbers a and b.
12 × 14 was just one instance of this pattern.
